## Design Context

### Users
Customer Success Managers (CSMs) at Aptum. They open the Renewals tab daily to work a pipeline of expiring contracts. Their job: ensure no renewal slips through unactioned. 90 days out = start the clock. 60 days = escalate. Expired = missed.

Three outcomes they're managing per customer:
1. Auto-renew flat
2. Roll in updated software/licensing pass-through fees (3rd-party costs Aptum pays and rebills)
3. M2M — same as #2 but +25% premium (Aptum prefers termed)

### Brand Personality
Professional, precise, time-aware. Internal tool for a telecom/managed services company. Not flashy. The UI should feel like a competent colleague, not a dashboard startup.

Three words: **Efficient. Urgent. Trustworthy.**

### Aesthetic Direction
- Dark-first (existing dark theme is the primary experience)
- Utilitarian dense tables — CSMs scan many rows quickly
- Geist font family (already in place, keep it)
- Accent palette already established: purple (#7D7AE8), orange (#FFBE81), red (#ef4444), green (#BED6A8)
- Yellow needed for 60–90 day warning band
- Anti-reference: no card grids, no hero metrics, no glassmorphism, no gradient text

### Design Principles
1. **Time is the primary data dimension** — expiration urgency should be immediately scannable; colors communicate deadline proximity without requiring mental math
2. **Density over decoration** — CSMs scan 100+ rows; every pixel should carry information
3. **Contract type is a primary filter** — the Termed/M2M distinction drives different workflows; it must be prominent and fast to switch
4. **States over pills** — inline color on text communicates urgency better than badges/pills for dates; save badges for categorical labels (M2M, MIXED)
5. **Default to the 90-day horizon** — that's the CSM's working window; default filters should show exactly what needs attention now
