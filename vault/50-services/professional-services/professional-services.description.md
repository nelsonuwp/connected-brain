## The Two Motions

Professional Services is organized into two motions with distinct delivery models, commercial structures, and success metrics.

### Motion 1: Advisory (Assess)

Structured assessments that diagnose the customer's environment, quantify risk, and produce a roadmap. Advisory is the tip of the spear. It builds trust, creates technical intimacy, and generates the evidence base that justifies the investment in Execute and Operate.

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| What it is | Seven structured assessments: Infrastructure Risk, Hybrid Cloud, Security Posture, Cloud Repatriation, Operational Maturity, Platform Modernization, Well-Architected Review |
| Commercial model | Fixed-fee, t-shirt sized (S: $5K-$7.5K, M: $10K-$15K, L: $20K-$25K, XL: $30K-$40K+) |
| Team model | SA-led, lightweight. S = SA only (20-40 hrs). M = SA + 1 specialist (50-90 hrs). L = SA + 2 specialists (100-170 hrs). XL = SA + 2-3 specialists + exec sponsor (200-300+ hrs). |
| Deliverable | Report with findings, risk scores, remediation roadmap, and cost estimates. The report is the sales tool for Execute and Operate. |
| Who scopes | SA using standardized sell sheets and deliverable scope templates (STG Assessment Playbook) |
| Who delivers | SA (from HSA team) with subject matter support from home teams as needed |
| Success metric | Follow-on conversion rate (target: 60-70% of delivered assessments produce a follow-on opportunity) |
| Revenue target (Year 1) | $140K-$207K from 11-14 assessments |

### Motion 2: Execute (Implement)

Project-based work that acts on assessment findings. Execute moves workloads, replaces hardware, remediates security gaps, builds platforms, and transitions operations. It is scoped by HSA, delivered by cross-functional teams, and handed off to operational teams.

| Dimension | Detail |
|---|---|
| What it is | Migrations, hardware refreshes, security remediations, platform builds, architecture redesigns, managed services transitions, DR implementations |
| Commercial model | SOW-scoped, milestone-based. Typical range: $15K-$300K per engagement. |
| Team model | Cross-functional. Resources drawn from home teams (Managed Cloud, Compute Platforms, DC Ops, Network, Service Desk) under dotted-line coordination. |
| Deliverable | Working environment, migrated workloads, hardened infrastructure, documented handoff to operational teams |
| Who scopes | HSA (Pat Wolthausen) architects define technical scope and budget hours |
| Who delivers | HSDM (Lacie Allen-Morley) coordinates cross-functional delivery |
| Success metric | On-time/on-budget delivery, clean handoff to Operate, SOW variance |
| Revenue (current) | $738K YTD (F26 Actual) |

## Accountable For

### Advisory Motion
- Maintaining and evolving the assessment portfolio (7 assessments, sell sheets, deliverable scope templates, delivery guides)
- Assessment pipeline tracking: pitched, sold, delivered, converted to follow-on
- Assessment delivery quality: consistency, timeliness, deliverable standards
- Follow-on conversion: percentage of delivered assessments that produce Execute or Operate opportunities
- Commercial team enablement: training AEs and SAs on assessment portfolio, t-shirt sizing, discovery questions, follow-on mapping

### Execute Motion
- Cross-functional delivery of non-recurring, project-based customer engagements
- Executing project deliverables according to the signed Statement of Work (SOW) within the agreed timeline and budget
- Resource coordination and dotted-line management of engineers seconded from home teams
- Maintaining engagement quality and consistency across disparate technical resources
- Structured, documented handoffs to Managed Cloud and Service Desk at project completion

## Problems We Solve

- Customers need a structured way to understand their environment before committing to infrastructure or managed services investments (Advisory solves this)
- Complex technical projects require cross-functional skills that do not exist on a single team (Execute solves this)
- Projects risk unexpected overruns or scope gaps without centralized delivery accountability (Execute process solves this)
- Home team managers risk having their operational capacity drained by unstructured project work (the Advisory/Execute distinction and resource approval process solve this)
- Operational teams (Managed Cloud, Service Desk) risk inheriting undocumented, unsupportable environments if project handoffs are poor (Execute handoff standards solve this)
- The commercial team needs an entry-point engagement that is lower commitment than a full SOW but higher value than a free discovery call (Advisory assessments solve this)

## Operating Model

### Advisory Operating Model

1. **Identify:** AE spots pain signal during customer conversation. Maps to StoryLeader question and corresponding assessment.
2. **Qualify:** AE uses sell sheet sizing questions to estimate t-shirt size. Validates fit with SA.
3. **Scope:** SA joins scoping call, refines scope, confirms deliverables, prepares SOW language from deliverable scope templates.
4. **Deliver:** SA leads execution with specialist support as needed. Follows standardized delivery guide for the specific assessment type.
5. **Present:** SA presents findings to customer. AE is in the room to identify follow-on signals.
6. **Convert:** AE proposes follow-on engagement (Execute project or Operate managed services) using assessment findings as the evidence base.

### Execute Operating Model

1. **Request & Approval:** HSDM identifies required skills and requests resources. The Home Team Service Manager must formally approve the draw to protect their operational commitments.
2. **Scoping:** HSA architects define the technical scope and estimate hours, using assessment findings (where available) as the baseline.
3. **Execution:** Seconded team members operate under a clearly defined scope for the duration of the engagement.
4. **Accountability:** The Home Team SM retains quality accountability for their seconded engineer's work, while HSDM owns overall project delivery and timeline.
5. **Handoff:** At project close, complete provisioning documentation and runbooks are handed off to operational teams. The handoff defines which managed service layers will take over day-2 operations.

## Identified Issues & Open Questions

- **Ownership Gap (Service Manager):** The owner of Professional Services is currently listed as "Open." With the formalization of two distinct motions (Advisory and Execute), this role becomes even more critical. The PS service manager needs to own assessment pipeline tracking, delivery quality, follow-on conversion metrics, and Execute delivery accountability. Without this role, the assessment framework risks being a set of documents that nobody drives.

- **Overlap with HSDM:** The accountabilities listed for Execute (owning SOW execution, drawing resources from home teams, dotted-line management) overlap with workflows currently owned by Lacie Allen-Morley under HSDM. It needs to be clarified whether the PS service manager role is distinct from HSDM or is a formalization of what HSDM already does, with the addition of Advisory pipeline ownership.

- **The Direct Labor Contradiction:** The operating model states PS is "not a standalone org unit with dedicated headcount." However, the financial sheet shows $485K of Actual Direct Labor sitting directly in the Professional Services column. If there is no dedicated headcount, whose salaries are making up this $485K? The Advisory/Execute formalization does not resolve this; it makes it more urgent to clarify.

- **Partner Services Budget Overrun:** The financial sheet shows an actual YTD Partner Services spend of $16K against a budget of only $2K. Are third-party contractors being utilized to fulfill project gaps because home team Service Managers are rejecting internal resource requests?

- **Assessment Enablement Gap:** The STG Assessment Playbook materials (sell sheets, delivery guides, deliverable scope templates) exist but have not been trained into the AE and SA teams. Phase 1 (Q2 2026) must include enablement sessions. Without this, the advisory motion exists on paper but not in practice.

- **Assessment Delivery Capacity:** HSA currently has Pat Wolthausen + 3 architects. If Year 1 targets 11-14 assessments at 20-300 hours each, the SA capacity question needs to be answered. Can HSA absorb assessment delivery alongside their existing pre-sales and Execute scoping responsibilities?

- **36-Account Matrix Validation:** The customer-to-assessment matrix pre-maps 36 priority accounts to primary and secondary assessments. This mapping needs validation with the AEs who own those relationships before assessments are pitched.
