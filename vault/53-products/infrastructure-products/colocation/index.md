# Colocation

Aptum colocation provides physical data center space — rack units, power (kW), cooling, and physical security — in Aptum-owned or Aptum-operated facilities. The customer brings their own hardware. Aptum provides the physical environment that keeps it running.

## What it is (and what it is not)

Colocation is physical hosting space for customer-owned hardware. It is not managed compute — Aptum does not provision, manage, or operate the hardware inside the rack. Network connectivity is a separate purchase from the Connectivity product. Managed services addons are available but limited; without hardware ownership, Aptum cannot manage the OS, patching, or application layers on customer-owned equipment without a separate agreement.

## What's always included (Fundamental guarantee)

Power delivered within specification, temperature within ASHRAE-compliant range, physical security maintained, and rack space available as contracted. Aptum Data Center Ops monitors environmental conditions and responds to physical incidents.

## How it's delivered

Manual provisioning by Data Center Ops. The customer arranges hardware delivery and Aptum handles rack installation, cabling, and power connection. Portal visibility into power consumption and bandwidth utilization is on the roadmap but not live today.

## Technical specifications

Rack units (RU) per cabinet, power delivered in kW increments, redundant cooling (N+1 CRAC units), UPS backup power with diesel generator failover (48-hour fuel runtime at primary sites), carrier-neutral meet-me-room access (15+ carriers at major Toronto and Herndon sites), and SOC 2 Type II certification at the Toronto Pullman data center.

## Pricing model

Priced per rack unit, per kW of power, and per circuit. Contract-based with standard term lengths. Power cost varies by data center location (illustrative rates: Herndon $110/kW, Atlanta $337/kW, Miami $48/kW, Los Angeles $457/kW, Toronto and Montreal $253/kW, Portsmouth $46/kW). Discount applies to margin component only.

## When this fits (and when it doesn't)

Colocation fits customers who own their hardware, require physical control over their equipment, and need a carrier-neutral facility for connectivity options. It also fits compliance and sovereignty use cases where the customer must retain custody of the physical hardware. It is not the right fit for customers who need Aptum to provision, manage, or replace compute on their behalf — those customers belong on Bare Metal or Aptum IaaS.

## Managed services available on top

Colo customers default to the Dedicated engagement tier (infrastructure Fundamentals only). The DDoS Protection and Hybrid Interconnects addons are available on colocation. Reviews and Touchpoints (Proactive tier only) is available for colo customers on a Proactive engagement. The full OS and application-layer addon catalog is not applicable to customer-owned hardware without a bespoke agreement.
