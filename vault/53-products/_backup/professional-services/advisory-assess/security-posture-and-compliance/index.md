> **For sales.** Use this to pitch, qualify, and size the assessment in a structured discovery conversation conversation.

---

## The One-Liner

**"Your firewall is end-of-life, your OS is unsupported, and you have unpatched firmware. If you got breached today, what would your auditor say?"**

## Who This Is For

- Companies in regulated industries (healthcare, finance, education, government-adjacent)
- Organizations with aging firewalls and unsupported operating systems
- Any customer that has been flagged in an audit or is preparing for one
- Businesses that handle sensitive data without a clear security posture

**Target personas**: CISO, VP of Security, Compliance Officer, IT Director, IT Manager

- "We know our firewalls are old but we haven't had budget to replace them"

## How to Size It (Questions to Ask)

### 1. "Do you know if any of your systems -- servers, firewalls, operating systems -- are past their support date or end-of-life?"
"I'm not sure" or "probably" = strong indicator. The answer itself doesn't size the deal, but "I don't know" on security questions = they definitely need this.

### 2. "Are you subject to any compliance requirements -- HIPAA, SOC 2, PCI, GDPR, anything like that? Has an auditor ever flagged your infrastructure?"
No compliance = S is likely enough. One framework = M. Multiple or recent audit findings = L/XL.

### 3. "How many servers and firewalls are in your environment?"
Under 5 = S. 5-15 = M. 15+ = L/XL.

### 4. "Do you have any public-facing systems -- a website, a customer portal, an API that partners connect to?"
Public-facing systems increase the attack surface and assessment scope.

### 5. "Has your company ever had a security incident, or is there a specific concern driving this -- like a board question, an audit, or something in the news?"
Reveals motivation. Incident or upcoming audit = high urgency.

### 6. "Do you have any security tools or a security team today, or is this being handled by your general IT team?"
No dedicated security = more foundational work needed.

### Quick Sizing Rule of Thumb
Count devices (same as infra risk). Then ask about compliance:
- **No compliance mandate** = Small
- **One compliance framework** = Medium
- **Multiple frameworks or "we need a pen test"** = Large
- **Enterprise, board-level, multi-framework** = XL

## Objection Handling

### "We've never been breached, so we must be fine."
*"That's what 90% of breached companies said the year before it happened. The question isn't whether you've been attacked -- it's whether you'd know if you were. And what your auditor or insurer would say."*

### "We can't afford this right now."
*"What's the cost of a breach? Average for mid-market is $2-5M. What's the cost of failing a compliance audit? The assessment is a rounding error compared to the risk it quantifies."*

### "Our IT team handles security."
*"Great. This assessment validates what they're doing and surfaces anything they might have missed. It also gives them a prioritized action plan they can take to leadership for budget."*

## What Happens After

The Security Posture Assessment typically leads to:

1. **Managed Cloud / OS & Above** -- ongoing patching, security monitoring, compliance maintenance
2. **Consulting** -- remediation project (firewall replacement, OS upgrades, security hardening)
3. **Infrastructure** -- replace EOL hardware (firewalls, servers)
4. **App Platform / DevOps** -- WAF implementation, security architecture
