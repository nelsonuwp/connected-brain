# Cloud Repatriation Assessment -- Sell Sheet

> **For sales.** Use this to pitch, qualify, and size the assessment in a structured discovery conversation conversation.

---

## The One-Liner

**"You moved to cloud 5 years ago. Your bills have doubled but your usage is flat. Let's figure out what should stay, what should come back, and how much you'd save."**

---

## Who This Is For

- Companies with significant public cloud spend ($10K+/month) that feels disproportionate to value
- Organizations that migrated to cloud 3-5+ years ago and are now seeing diminishing returns
- Businesses with stable, predictable workloads still running on elastic (expensive) cloud infrastructure
- Any customer showing "cloud fatigue" -- rising costs, declining support quality, vendor lock-in concerns

**Target personas**: VP Infrastructure, CTO, CIO, CFO, Head of Cloud Strategy, FinOps lead

## How to Size It (Questions to Ask)

### 1. "Do you have a rough idea what you're spending on cloud per month? Even a ballpark -- under $10K, $10-50K, over $50K?"

This is the #1 sizing input. Under $10K = S. $10-20K = M. $20-100K = L. Over $100K = XL.

### 2. "Which cloud provider are you using -- AWS, Azure, Google, or a mix?"

Single provider = simpler. Multi-cloud = bump up.

### 3. "Do you know which cloud services you're using? Like, is it mostly virtual machines, or are you using things like managed databases, serverless functions, or other specialized services?"

"Mostly VMs" = easier to repatriate. "We use a lot of managed services" = more lock-in analysis needed.

### 4. "Has your cloud bill been going up even though your usage feels about the same?"

This is the emotional hook. If yes, lean in hard.

### 5. "If you moved some things out of cloud, do you have your own data center or hosting to move them to, or would that need to be figured out?"

Existing colo/on-prem = simpler (cost comparison). No existing landing zone = assessment includes target architecture design.

### 6. "When is your cloud contract up for renewal?"

If within 6 months, urgency is high. The assessment needs to happen NOW.

### Quick Sizing Rule of Thumb

Ask what they spend on cloud:

- **Under $10K/month** = Small
- **$10-20K/month** = Medium
- **$20-100K/month** = Large
- **Over $100K/month** = XL\
  Adjust up if they use multiple cloud providers.

## Objection Handling

### "Repatriation sounds like going backwards."

*"It's not about going back to 2015. It's about using modern private infrastructure for workloads that don't need the cloud. Companies like Dropbox saved $75M over two years. 37signals cut cloud by 60%. It's the new playbook for mature companies."*

### "The migration effort isn't worth it."

*"That's exactly what the assessment determines. We model the cost of migrating against the cost of staying. If the math doesn't work, we'll tell you. But for most companies with stable workloads, the payback is 4-6 months."*

### "We just signed a 3-year cloud contract."

*"Even better time to start planning. You have 2+ years to execute methodically. And the assessment gives you leverage for your next negotiation -- a credible alternative is the best negotiating tool."*

### "What about our cloud-native workloads?"

*"Repatriation isn't all-or-nothing. The assessment identifies what should stay in cloud and what should move. The goal is the right workload in the right place."*
