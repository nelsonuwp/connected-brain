# Infrastructure Risk & Readiness Assessment -- Deliverable Scope

> **SOW-ready language.** Copy the relevant size section into the Statement of Work.

---

## Assessment Description

The Infrastructure Risk & Readiness Assessment provides a comprehensive evaluation of the customer's current infrastructure environment, identifying end-of-life hardware, unsupported software, operational risks, and capacity gaps. The assessment produces a prioritized remediation roadmap with cost estimates, enabling informed decision-making about infrastructure refresh, modernization, or migration.

---

## Objective

To assess the current state of the customer's infrastructure, identify critical risks and vulnerabilities related to aging or unsupported components, and deliver actionable recommendations for remediation, prioritized by risk severity and business impact.

---

## T-Shirt Sizing

### Small ($5K) -- Single-Site, Limited Scope

**Scope**: 1-3 services/devices, single data center or hosting environment.

**Deliverables**:

- Hardware inventory with age and EOL status for all in-scope devices
- Operating system lifecycle report (supported, approaching EOL, unsupported)
- Top-10 prioritized risk findings with severity ratings
- Summary recommendations document (5-8 pages)

**Duration**: 1 week

**Aptum team**: Solution Architect (solo)

**Customer time commitment**: 1-2 hours (initial data share + brief interview)

---

### Medium ($10K) -- Standard Assessment

**Scope**: 4-10 services/devices, single site, may include mixed managed and colo environments.

**Deliverables**:

- Full hardware age audit and EOL component inventory (CPUs, SSDs, NICs, firewalls)
- Operating system lifecycle status report for all in-scope systems
- Operational risk scoring based on ping loss patterns, ticket history, and incident frequency
- Capacity and performance baseline analysis
- Full prioritized remediation roadmap with cost estimates
- Assessment report (15-25 pages)

**Duration**: 2 weeks

**Aptum team**: Solution Architect + 1 specialist (network or infrastructure)

**Customer time commitment**: 3-5 hours (kickoff + interviews + data access)

---

### Large ($20K) -- Multi-Site or Complex Environment

**Scope**: 10+ services/devices, multi-site or complex mixed environments (on-prem, colo, managed hosting).

**Deliverables**:

- Everything in Medium, plus:
- Multi-site infrastructure topology mapping
- Cross-site dependency analysis
- Detailed remediation roadmap with phased execution plan and timeline
- Cost/benefit analysis for each remediation phase
- Executive summary presentation (slide deck)
- Assessment report (25-40 pages)

**Duration**: 3-4 weeks

**Aptum team**: Solution Architect + 2 specialists (network, infrastructure, or security)

**Customer time commitment**: 6-10 hours (kickoff + multiple stakeholder interviews + data access + findings review)

---

### XL ($30K+) -- Enterprise / Bespoke

**Scope**: Enterprise-scale, multiple sites and environments, full organizational infrastructure assessment. Custom SOW required.

**Deliverables**:

- Everything in Large, plus:
- Full infrastructure asset register with lifecycle forecasting
- Business impact analysis for each critical risk
- Remediation roadmap with executive-level business case and ROI modeling
- Board-ready executive presentation
- Stakeholder workshop to align on remediation priorities
- Assessment report (40+ pages, customized format)

**Duration**: 4-6 weeks

**Aptum team**: Solution Architect + 2-3 specialists + Aptum executive sponsor

**Customer time commitment**: 10-20 hours (multiple workshops, interviews, data sessions, review cycles)

---

## Out of Scope

The following are explicitly excluded unless added via SOW amendment:

- Penetration testing or active vulnerability exploitation (see Security Posture Assessment)
- Application-level assessment or code review
- Cloud environment assessment (see Hybrid Cloud or Cloud Repatriation Assessment)
- Remediation implementation (this is a follow-on engagement)
- Procurement of replacement hardware
- Compliance gap analysis against specific frameworks (see Security Posture Assessment)

---

## Customer Responsibilities

The customer is responsible for:

1. **Access**: Providing remote or on-site access to infrastructure environments as needed
1. **Data**: Sharing existing asset inventories, network diagrams, and monitoring data (if available)
1. **Availability**: Making key stakeholders available for interviews and workshops within the agreed timeline
1. **Credentials**: Providing read-only access to management interfaces, monitoring tools, and ticketing systems
1. **Point of contact**: Designating a single technical point of contact for the duration of the assessment

---

## Assumptions

- Customer will provide access to all in-scope environments within 3 business days of kickoff
- Existing documentation (network diagrams, asset lists) will be shared at kickoff; if none exists, discovery effort may increase
- Assessment findings are based on data available at the time of assessment; conditions may change
- Remediation cost estimates are budgetary ranges, not firm quotes
- The assessment does not include implementation of any recommendations

---

## Acceptance Criteria

The assessment is considered complete when:

1. All deliverables for the selected size have been provided to the customer
1. A findings presentation has been delivered to the customer's designated stakeholders
1. The customer has had the opportunity to ask questions and receive clarifications
1. The final assessment report has been delivered in PDF format
