# Managed Services

Aptum's managed services stack on top of any infrastructure commodity. The same addons are available whether the customer is on colocation, bare metal, Aptum IaaS, or public cloud. What changes across products is addon compatibility, not the service itself.

## How the catalog is organized

Every Aptum customer relationship has three layers. Infrastructure commodities are the base: compute, storage, networking, and cloud access. Every commodity includes a Fundamental guarantee that the infrastructure works as sold. This cannot be removed or priced separately.

Engagement tiers sit on top of the commodity and determine the support relationship and the operational posture Aptum takes. Every customer is on one of three tiers.

Managed service addons are purchasable services that stack on top of any commodity. Each addon is one product with two modes of operation determined by tier. The addon is the same product either way — what changes is who holds operational responsibility for it.

## The three engagement tiers

The tier ladder is deliberate. Customers who start at Dedicated can move to Reactive or Proactive as their trust in and reliance on Aptum grows. The tier model is not a segment wall — it is a sales motion.

**Dedicated (Infrastructure)** is infrastructure-only with no managed services. Aptum keeps the infrastructure running and nothing more. Customers handle everything above the infrastructure layer themselves: OS, patching, security, backups, application management. Professional Services is available on a pay-as-you-go basis for tasks above the floor. Addon access: none. Not available on Bare Metal or Private Cloud, where the Service Desk layer is mandatory and non-removable.

**Reactive (Co-Managed)** means Aptum operates the environment reactively while the customer retains oversight and decision-making authority. This is Aptum's co-managed posture. 24/7 Service Desk coverage. Standard monitoring with customer dashboard access. Reactive support of the OS, networking, and storage layer. L2 triage included. The customer receives alerts, approves patch windows, reviews job status, and requests changes via ticket. Aptum responds; the customer decides. This tier fits two customer profiles equally: IT teams using Aptum as augmentation (Aptum covers the tooling and after-hours load while their team focuses on strategic work), and organizations without internal IT teams who stay in the approval loop.

**Proactive (Fully Managed)** means Aptum owns and operates the environment. The customer does not need to be in the routine operational loop. Aptum anticipates problems, designs policy, manages changes, and delivers outcomes. Named Managed Cloud resource. Scheduled monthly or quarterly environment reviews included at no additional cost. Proactive event management. BCP Planning and Compliance Reporting are available at this tier only.

Working names versus customer-facing names: Dedicated maps to "Infrastructure," Reactive maps to "Co-Managed," and Proactive maps to "Fully Managed."

## Reviews and Touchpoints

Structured cadence reviews — where Aptum reviews environment health, ticket patterns, and upcoming activities with the customer — are included in the Proactive tier at no additional cost. This is what Proactive looks like in practice: a named MC resource who prepares and runs regular sessions, surfaces patterns, and proactively plans ahead. Reviews and Touchpoints is not a separately purchasable addon; it is part of what the Proactive tier means.

## Assessment findings map to tier recommendations

Every assessment produces findings that map to a specific tier recommendation. Infrastructure Risk and Hybrid Cloud assessments typically lead to Reactive (monitoring and OS patching as starting addons). Security Posture, Cloud Repatriation, and Operational Maturity assessments typically lead to Proactive (compliance reporting is Proactive-only; full stack ownership is the goal). App and Platform Modernization and Well-Architected Review can land at either Reactive or Proactive depending on the customer's IT maturity.

## The addon catalog

Addons are purchasable managed services priced per endpoint or asset per month. They are organized into nine categories in the Addons section below: Monitoring and Observability, OS and Platform Management, Data Protection and Recovery, Security Services, Cloud and Hybrid Connectivity, Logging, Network and Delivery, Application Services, and Managed Productivity. Each category defines the specific named Aptum products within it and includes a service guide covering delivery across all products in that group.
