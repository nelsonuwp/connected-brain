Aptum Product Strategy — External Review

Prepared as outside-in consulting feedback · 2026-05-18 · Reviewing 74 pages under 53-products/

Bottom line. The strategy is intellectually sound and the ICP is sharper than most mid-market MSPs we see. But the catalog cannot currently be sold or delivered at the quality the strategy promises. The two valuation drags are operational, not strategic: (1) 19 of ~30 service guides are placeholder stubs, and (2) the path from 6.5% managed-services penetration to the 15–20% industry norm is implied but not published as a measurable plan. Fix those two and the same business presents to an acquirer at a materially different multiple.

The biggest hidden risk is naming and brand coherence: the PRD still uses "Apt Cloud", the strategy uses "Aptum Portal", and customer-facing materials will inherit the confusion. This is a one-week fix that disproportionately affects perceived maturity.

The biggest hidden opportunity is the channel/reseller motion you've explicitly deprioritized. ES Williams is a working proof point; deprioritizing it removes the multiplier that justifies the Aptum Portal investment.

3 · Market alignment — outside-in view

Tailwinds Aptum is correctly leaning into

  • Broadcom/VMware refugee market — Gartner says 35% migration by 2028. Aptum's CloudStack + Proxmox + Dedicated Cloud answer is on-message and uncommon.
  • Cloud repatriation — 21% of orgs have repatriated workloads (validated trend). Aptum's "right workload on the right platform" matches the buyer's worldview.
  • Mid-market squeeze — Between hyperscaler complexity and small MSP capacity, mid-market buyers are looking for a partner that's bigger than their MSP but smaller than Accenture. Aptum sits there.
  • Canadian sovereignty — Canadian buyers and US East regulated buyers will pay 10–20% premium for the SOC 2 Type II Toronto presence.

Tailwinds Aptum is missing

  • AI infrastructure — not playing. See open question below.
  • FinOps / cloud cost optimization as a standalone service — buyers ask for this by name. Well-Architected Review is adjacent but not packaged this way.
  • Zero Trust / SASE — adjacent to Managed Firewall + Managed Detection and Response. Not in the catalog as a named offering. Buyers in regulated industries lead with this.
  • Compliance-as-an-outcome (SOC 2, HIPAA, PCI prep & evidence) — Aptum has the substrate (SOC 2 Type II DC), but doesn't sell a "we'll get you SOC 2 ready" project.

Headwinds Aptum is correctly avoiding

  • Hyperscaler resale at a loss (Ian Rae's explicit call) — correct.
  • Standalone B2B2B portal SaaS sale — correct.
  • Large-enterprise managed services pursuit — correct.
  • Pure commodity hosting backfill — correct (let it attrit).

Headwinds Aptum should re-think

  • Treating MSP channel as Phase 2 work. ES Williams is already live. The motion is here, not later.
  • Treating Advisory enterprise pursuit as out-of-scope. Big-company assessment is a different P&L from managed services and shouldn't be conflated.
  • De-emphasizing Apt Cloud / Aptum Portal as a sellable product. Correct that it's not B2B2B SaaS, but the portal experience itself is a buying reason — sell the experience even if you don't sell the SKU.

5 · Open questions and recommended answers

Q1 · What is Aptum's AI infrastructure position?

Three viable answers; pick one within 30 days, commit publicly.

Recommended: "Partner-led AI infrastructure, Aptum-managed." Aptum doesn't buy GPUs. Aptum partners with one or two GPU-cloud providers (5C.ai is the obvious candidate; Latitude.sh adjacent) and bundles them into Aptum-managed offerings: customer buys GPU capacity through Aptum, gets Aptum managed services (monitoring, patching, security) wrapped around it. Same playbook as the hyperscaler resale motion. Aptum becomes the AI-adjacent managed provider, not the AI infrastructure provider. Lowest capex, fits the brand, defensible.
Alternative A: "Explicit out-of-scope, refer out." Cleanest. Aptum tells customers it doesn't do AI infrastructure and refers them. Loses the conversation but loses no money.
Alternative B: "Build it." Capex-heavy. Not consistent with the rest of the strategy. Don't.

Q2 · What is the path from 6.5% to 15% managed-services penetration?

The strategy asserts the target but doesn't publish the plan.

Recommended: Four-quarter program with three workstreams.
(1) Renewal-tied assessments: every renewal in the 72% expiring-within-6-months wave gets an assessment offer as conversation opener. Target: 80 assessments offered, 30 sold per quarter.
(2) Top-50 hosting-only campaign: AE-led outbound on the top 50 hosting-only accounts ($659K/mo total). Target: 20 Infrastructure Risk assessments per quarter, 50% conversion to L2 managed OS.
(3) Multi-LOB deepening: 135 multi-LOB customers get a quarterly portfolio review with named CSM. Target: 1 new addon per multi-LOB customer per year.
End-of-2026 outcome at 50% conversion: ~$200K/mo new MRR, takes penetration to ~12%.

Q3 · Should the MSP channel be a named third pillar?

Yes. The portal infrastructure exists, the motion has a proof point (ES Williams), and the alternative use of the portal asset is suboptimal.

Recommended: Rename the two-pillar story to three pillars in the strategy doc: Managed Services, Aptum IaaS, MSP Channel. Channel pillar gets a named owner (BD lead), a recruit target (3 MSPs in 2026), and a revenue target (channel MRR as a % of new ARR). Use ES Williams as the testimonial. This is the highest-leverage rewrite in the strategy.

Q4 · How should "Apt Cloud" vs "Aptum Portal" be resolved?

The strategy doc has resolved this ("Aptum Portal" is the customer-facing name). The PRD has not.

Recommended: One-pass edit of the PRD. Aptum Portal everywhere customer-facing. Glossary entry preserves "Apt Cloud" as historical. Done in a day.

Q5 · When does Alert Logic MDR commercial model get resolved?

Listed as "IN DEVELOPMENT". Until resolved, the entire Security Posture follow-on path has a soft spot.

Recommended: Lock the partnership shape with Alert Logic by end of Q2 2026. Two clean options: (a) Aptum operates the SOC with Alert Logic tooling — higher margin, requires Tier 2/3 security ops capacity; (b) Alert Logic operates the SOC, Aptum bundles and markets — lower margin, faster to ship. (b) is the right answer for 2026 with (a) as a 2027 maturity path.

Q6 · Should Build-Operate-Transfer remain explicitly rejected?

The reasoning (forever Operate is the goal) is right. But BOT is a commercial wrapper, not a motion.

Recommended: Soften the language. BOT is offered when the customer asks; it's not a goal Aptum sells. Reframe in one paragraph in "What We Stop Doing".

Q7 · What is the long-tail data center footprint strategy?

26 sites is positioned as a feature; only 6 carry meaningful revenue.

Recommended: Name 6 Tier 1 sites (Herndon, Toronto, Portsmouth, Atlanta, Miami, LA). All new product investment goes there. Long-tail sites get a quiet 3-year sunset/consolidation plan, not announced externally. Use the savings to fund SOC 2 expansion at the Tier 1 sites.

Q8 · What's the FinOps offering?

Cloud cost optimization is asked for by name in mid-market RFPs. Well-Architected Review is adjacent but not packaged as FinOps.

Recommended: Carve a new advisory deliverable from Well-Architected: "FinOps Assessment". S/M sizing ($5K/$10K). Reuse the methodology, change the framing and the deliverable templates. Two weeks of packaging work; new doors open immediately. If carving out feels too much, position Well-Architected as "Well-Architected + FinOps" in marketing copy.

7 · What I would tell a board

Aptum has the right thesis, an unusually disciplined ICP, and a top-decile gross margin profile on its core infrastructure product. The Broadcom and repatriation tailwinds are real and Aptum is on-message.

What's holding the valuation back today is operational packaging, not strategic clarity: 19 service guides are stubs, no SLA framework is published, the channel motion is treated as future when it's already proven, and the 6.5%-to-15% penetration target is asserted without a published plan. None of these are hard to fix. Most can be done in 60 days with the existing team.

The single highest-ROI 60-day investment is publishing six service guides with SLAs and a rate card. The single highest-ROI strategic edit is promoting the MSP channel from Phase 2 work to a named third revenue pillar. Both are paperwork-grade tasks with multi-million-dollar valuation consequences.
