Aptum Product Strategy — External Review

External consulting review · 2026-05-18 · Full audit of 74 pages under vault/53-products

Bottom line. The strategy is intellectually sound and the ICP is sharper than most mid-market MSPs we see. The deeper read reinforces this: the seven advisory assessments are well-structured, the infrastructure product pages carry real Fundamentals guarantees, and the addon catalog defines specific named products. Where Aptum loses is operationalization. Strategy says many things that the catalog cannot yet deliver, and there are concrete consistency defects that an acquirer's diligence team will find in their first pass.

The four most material gaps: (1) 19 service guides are placeholder stubs with no SLAs, pricing, or delivery procedures; (2) the path from 6.5% to 15% managed-services penetration is asserted as a roadmap line, not a published quarterly plan; (3) the MSP/reseller channel motion is positioned as Phase 2 future work despite a working ES Williams proof point — this is the highest-leverage strategy rewrite available; (4) the Aptum IaaS GTM that the board explicitly directed as a "fast follow" in March has no published commercial playbook, target list, or quoting motion.

Consistency defects you should know about: (a) tier-availability contradiction between managed-services/index.md and infrastructure-products/aptum-iaas-private-cloud/index.md on whether Dedicated tier exists on Private Cloud; (b) multiple addon products listed as available but status "IN DEVELOPMENT" or "not yet built" (MDR, Vulnerability Scanning, Compliance Reporting, Database Tuning, DevOps Monitoring); (c) the App and Platform Modernization delivery guide names specific vendor tools (GitHub Actions, GitLab CI, ArgoCD) in customer-facing language, violating the strategy's vendor-neutral commitment; (d) public-cloud product pages use the internal name "CloudOps Software" rather than the customer-facing "Aptum Portal"; (e) the PRD still uses "Apt Cloud" throughout while the strategy uses "Aptum Portal".

What an acquirer pays for that you already have: 74–89% gross margins on the core IaaS product (top decile for the category), a disciplined single ICP that excludes the right buyers, a market-aligned position on the Broadcom and repatriation trends, SOC 2 Type II Canadian sovereignty, and a working channel proof point in ES Williams. None of these are advertised at the volume they deserve.

4 · Market alignment

Tailwinds Aptum is correctly leaning into

  • Broadcom/VMware refugee market — Gartner: 35% migration by 2028. Aptum's CloudStack + Proxmox + Dedicated Cloud answer is on-message and uncommon. The PRD's make-vs-buy analysis lands the case cleanly.
  • Cloud repatriation — 21% of orgs have repatriated. Aptum's "right workload on the right platform" matches the buyer's worldview.
  • Mid-market squeeze — between hyperscaler complexity and small MSP capacity. Aptum sits there.
  • Canadian sovereignty — Canadian and US East regulated buyers will pay 10–20% premium for SOC 2 Type II Toronto presence.
  • "Single portal, single bill, single governance" proposition — this is the most resonant customer-facing line in the catalog. Use it more.

Tailwinds Aptum is missing

  • AI infrastructure — not playing. See §6 Q1.
  • FinOps as a standalone offering — buyers ask for this by name. Well-Architected Review is adjacent but not packaged as FinOps.
  • Zero Trust / SASE — adjacent to Managed Firewall + MDR. Not a named offering. Regulated industries lead with this.
  • Compliance-as-an-outcome (SOC 2, HIPAA, PCI prep & evidence) — Aptum has the substrate (SOC 2 Type II DC) but no packaged "we'll get you ready" project.
  • Managed Kubernetes as a product — Kubernetes is roadmap on the platform but not yet a named managed-services offering. Modern mid-market increasingly asks for it.

Headwinds Aptum is correctly avoiding

  • Hyperscaler resale at a loss — correct.
  • Standalone B2B2B portal SaaS sale — correct.
  • Large-enterprise managed services pursuit — correct.
  • Pure commodity hosting backfill — correct (let it attrit).
  • Switching off CloudStack onto OpenStack — correct (the PRD's analysis is sound).

Headwinds Aptum should re-think

  • Treating MSP channel as Phase 2. ES Williams is live. The motion is here.
  • Excluding enterprise from Advisory. Big-company assessments are a different P&L from managed services and shouldn't be conflated.
  • De-emphasizing the Aptum Portal as a sellable product. Correct that it's not B2B2B SaaS — but the portal experience itself is a buying reason. Sell the experience even if you don't sell the SKU.
  • Inertia on the legacy "Aptum Cloud" customer migration. The 38 customers / $27K MRC on VMware are still on the cost line and described as Stream 3 / Planned. With Broadcom prices climbing, every quarter of delay is a margin tax.

6 · Open questions and recommended answers

Q1 · What is Aptum's AI infrastructure position?

Three viable answers; pick one in 30 days and commit publicly.

Recommended: "Partner-led AI infrastructure, Aptum-managed." Aptum doesn't buy GPUs. Aptum partners with one or two GPU-cloud providers (5C.ai is the obvious candidate; Latitude.sh adjacent) and bundles them into Aptum-managed offerings. Same playbook as hyperscaler resale. Aptum becomes the AI-adjacent managed provider, not the AI infrastructure provider. Lowest capex, fits the brand, defensible.
Alternative A: "Explicit out-of-scope, refer out." Cleanest. Loses the conversation but loses no money.
Alternative B: "Build it." Capex-heavy. Inconsistent with the rest of the strategy. Don't.

Q2 · What is the path from 6.5% to 15% managed-services penetration?

The strategy asserts the target but doesn't publish the plan.

Recommended: Four-quarter program with three workstreams.
(1) Renewal-tied assessments: every renewal in the 72%-expiring-within-6-months wave gets an assessment offer as conversation opener. Target: 80 assessments offered, 30 sold per quarter.
(2) Top-50 hosting-only campaign: AE-led outbound on the top 50 hosting-only accounts ($659K/mo total). Target: 20 Infrastructure Risk assessments per quarter, 50% conversion to L2 Managed OS.
(3) Multi-LOB deepening: 135 multi-LOB customers get a quarterly portfolio review with named CSM. Target: 1 new addon per multi-LOB customer per year.
End-of-2026 outcome at 50% conversion: ~$200K/mo new MRR, takes penetration to ~12%.

Q3 · Should the MSP channel be a named third pillar?

Yes. The portal infrastructure exists, the motion has a proof point (ES Williams), and the alternative use of the portal asset is suboptimal.

Recommended: Rename the two-pillar story to three pillars in the strategy doc: Managed Services, Aptum IaaS, MSP Channel. Channel pillar gets a named owner (BD lead), a recruit target (3 MSPs in 2026), and a revenue target (channel MRR as % of new ARR). Use ES Williams as testimonial. This is the highest-leverage rewrite in the strategy.

Q4 · How is "Apt Cloud" vs "Aptum Portal" resolved?

The strategy doc has resolved this; the PRD and Aptum Portal Strategy and Roadmap have not.

Recommended: One-pass edit of the PRD and the strategy-and-roadmap doc. Aptum Portal everywhere customer-facing. Glossary entry preserves "Apt Cloud" as historical. CloudOps Software referenced only when discussing the underlying platform supplier, never in customer-facing pages.

Q5 · When does Alert Logic MDR commercial model get resolved?

Listed as "IN DEVELOPMENT". Until resolved, the entire Security Posture follow-on path has a soft spot.

Recommended: Lock the partnership shape by end of Q2 2026. Two options: (a) Aptum operates the SOC with Alert Logic tooling — higher margin, requires Tier 2/3 security ops capacity; (b) Alert Logic operates the SOC, Aptum bundles and markets — lower margin, faster to ship. (b) is the right answer for 2026 with (a) as a 2027 maturity path.

Q6 · What is the status of MDR, Vulnerability Scanning, Compliance Reporting, Database Tuning, DevOps Monitoring?

All five appear in the public addon catalog with ambiguous status ("IN DEVELOPMENT", "not yet built", "PS-led today", "evolving"). Sales positioning is unclear.

Recommended: Decision meeting this week. For each addon: classify as Buyable Now / Buyable Q3 / Not Buyable / Custom PS Only. Update each addon page with explicit status pill and (where applicable) target-availability quarter. Two-hour meeting, one-day documentation pass.

Q7 · What is the Aptum IaaS GTM plan that the board directed?

The board mandate from March 31 is a "fast follow" — no published commercial plan yet.

Recommended: Single-page Aptum IaaS GTM plan as a new document in the strategy folder: target segments, assessment-to-IaaS funnel, pricing vs. AWS/Azure positioning, AE enablement schedule, named GTM owner, 30/60/90-day milestones. Ship in 14 days. The competition is doing this; Aptum's window on the Broadcom wave is finite.

Q8 · Should Build-Operate-Transfer remain explicitly rejected?

Reasoning (forever Operate is the goal) is right. But BOT is a commercial wrapper, not a motion.

Recommended: Soften the language. BOT is offered when the customer asks; it's not a goal Aptum sells. One-paragraph edit in "What We Stop Doing".

Q9 · What is the long-tail data center footprint strategy?

26 sites positioned as a feature; only 6 carry meaningful revenue.

Recommended: Name 6 Tier 1 sites (Herndon, Toronto, Portsmouth, Atlanta, Miami, LA). All new product investment goes there. Long-tail sites get a quiet 3-year sunset/consolidation plan, not announced externally. Fund SOC 2 expansion at Tier 1 sites with the savings.

Q10 · What's the FinOps offering?

Cloud cost optimization is asked by name in mid-market RFPs. Well-Architected Review is adjacent but not packaged this way.

Recommended: Carve a "FinOps Assessment" deliverable from Well-Architected. S/M sizing ($5K/$10K). Reuse the methodology, change the framing. Two weeks of packaging work; new doors open immediately. Alternative: position Well-Architected as "Well-Architected + FinOps" in marketing copy without a new SKU.

Q11 · Customer-facing tier names — keep both or pick one?

"Dedicated/Reactive/Proactive" (working) and "Infrastructure/Co-Managed/Fully Managed" (customer-facing) are both used in different parts of the catalog.

Recommended: Use "Reactive" and "Proactive" as customer-facing names — they describe the operational posture clearly and are already used throughout delivery docs. Drop "Co-Managed" and "Fully Managed" as marketing synonyms. Update the managed-services index to remove the mapping table. Two-paragraph rewrite.

Q12 · What happens with the 38 legacy Aptum Cloud / $27K MRC customers on VMware?

Documented as Stream 3 / Planned. With Broadcom pricing climbing, the timing matters.

Recommended: Publish a transition plan with named owner and quarterly cohort targets. These customers are the easiest Aptum IaaS sales in the entire portfolio — they're already on Aptum, they're already paying, and Broadcom is making the case for them. Move them faster.
