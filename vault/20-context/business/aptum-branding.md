# Aptum Brand Identity Guidelines (2024)

## 1. Brand Strategy

* **Mission**: Empower businesses to thrive in the moment through transformative technology solutions and expert guidance.
* **Brand Idea**: Presence in the present.
* **Positioning Statement**: Aptum is a strategic technology partner leveraging a history of deep expertise to deliver tailored solutions that empower costumers to navigate change confidently.
* **Brand Behaviors**:
  * People first, partners always.
  * Theory and practice.
  * Clarity in fluidity.
  * Future meets fundamental.
  * Stewards of community.

## 2. Verbal Identity (Our Voice)

* **Voice Attitude**: The brand voice embodies a "Trade Co-operative" attitude—an epicenter for transmitting knowledge, sharing resources, and succeeding together rather than just selling.
* **Rallying Cry**: "People first, partners always."
* **Core Voice Principles**:
  * Principle 01: We are real people serving real people.
  * Principle 02: We keep it plain and simple.
  * Principle 03: We prioritize action.
* **Tone**: Practical, clear, and accessible.

## 3. Visual Identity

### Logo

* **Symbol**: Two overlapping rings representing a distillation of Aptum and its partners coming together.
* **Lockups**: The Aptum logotype should always be used with the ring symbol in either the horizontal or vertical logo. The brand symbol serves as the central icon across social media platforms and various digital channels.
* **Color Rules**: The logotype is primarily set in only black or white. In special use cases where the same logo file needs to be used on a light and dark background, such as an email signature, the entire logo may be set in lavender.
* **Clear Space**: Equal to the space between the symbol and the wordmark (or 1/4th the symbol height for the symbol alone).

### Color Palette

* **Proportions**: To ensure ample white space for content at least 50% of an application should be clear of color. Proportions are roughly ≥ 50% White, 15% Primary Lavender, 30% Deep Navy & Neutrals, and ≤5% Accent & Accent Gradients.

### Typography

* **Primary Typeface**: *Geist*. Larger scale Light and Regular weights commanding attention as headlines, while Medium and Regular weights maintain clarity for body text and captions.
* **Supporting Typeface**: *Geist Mono* for small use-cases such as eyebrow labels and numerals in charts and graphs.
* **System/Fallback Fonts**: When operating within Google Suite, use Inter in place of Geist. For PowerPoint documents that need to be partner-editable, use Helvetica.
* **Formatting Rules**: Our text is consistently left aligned. By default, our typeface should have 0% tracking unless specified otherwise. Do not set headlines or supporting copy in all caps.

### Photography

* **Art Direction Principles**: Confident, Authentic, Crafted, and Engaged.
* **Editing/Filters**: Adjust the coloring to feel neutral and slightly cool-toned. A Lavender filter can also be added to neutralize anything too bright.
* **Restrictions**: Do not use black and white photography. Do not use brightly saturated photography. Do not use overly staged or posed photography.

### Iconography & Illustration

* **Level 1 (Functional Iconography)**: Utilitarian and simple, our functional Iconography forms the base of our icon system. Sourced from the Remix icon library and brought into our brand style with a gradient overlay.
* **Level 2 (Illustrative Iconography)**: Illustrative icons are used to represent complex concepts. Our icons simplify these concepts by layering two base icons. For dark icons, use neutral gray 3 as the base color with the dark gradient applied. For light icons, use white as the base color with the light gradient applied.
* **Level 3 (Partnership Rings)**: We can use the rings in 2D, 3D, and stylized with a gradient. Uses include: Image container shape, Background texture, and Clarity focal point.
