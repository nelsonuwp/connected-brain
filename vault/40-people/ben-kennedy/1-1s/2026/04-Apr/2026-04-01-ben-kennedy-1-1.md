### 3. Capacity Framework: Network Dimensions (10 min)
Ben already flagged that network capacity is multidimensional ("x1, x2, etc. and z1, z2, etc."). That's fine — start with the headline numbers.

Proposed starting dimensions:

**Port capacity:**
- Total ports available by type (MPLS, internet, cross-connect) per location
- Ports consumed / committed
- Available ports before procurement

**Bandwidth:**
- Total bandwidth purchased (transit)
- Bandwidth committed to customers
- Available bandwidth

**IP addresses:**
- Total IP blocks owned
- IPs allocated to customers
- IPs available for sale/lease

Ask him:
- Are these the right starting dimensions, or are there others that matter more?
- Where does this data live today?
- Can he produce a first-pass view, even rough? Target Mon Apr 7.
- Which locations are capacity-constrained? Which have headroom?
