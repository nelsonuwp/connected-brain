# Expert Services Economic Model

## The Raw Idea
Need an economic model for Expert Services (Professional Services, Managed Services, and Hybrid) that proves we know exactly how our operational engine works.

## Why This Matters
- The CEO has asked for this "7 times 7 different ways" and wants to see that I have a firm grip on the "physics" of the department—how work comes in, how it's executed, and how it translates to profit.    
- We are reverse-engineering an October transaction. Buyers and board members don't just buy revenue; they buy scalable engines. If we can't cleanly articulate our unit economics and operating leverage (i.e., we don't need to hire a new body for every new dollar of revenue), the valuation takes a hit.    

## What I Think I Want
A two-part "Operating Manual" for Expert Services:
1. **The Scoreboard (The Math):** A clean financial model showing our theoretical capacity (~$9.6M) vs. baseline costs (~$3.2M), target gross margins by service line, and our actual utilization rates.    
2. **The Playbook (The Workflow):** A clear, visual value stream map that proves governance. It needs to show exactly how work enters the funnel (Intake/Triage), how it gets assigned to the Golden Sheet without breaking people, and how we measure the output.    

## What I Don't Know Yet
- Exactly how much of our theoretical ~$9.6M capacity is "leaking" through non-billable bench time, scope creep, or poor sales handoffs.
- How to cleanly map our Managed Hosting/NOC fixed-fee MRR to the actual hours those teams are burning, to ensure we aren't over-servicing clients at a loss.
- How to visualize the "Intake & Triage" process for the CEO without making it look like a convoluted bowl of spaghetti.
- Whether the 15% PM/SDM overhead structure is actually covering the cost of those roles in reality versus just in the spreadsheet.
## What I'm Conflating
- **The Financial P&L vs. The Operational Workflow:** I was initially focused on just giving him the financial model (the output). He is actually asking for the governance model (the inputs, triage, and allocation) _that leads_ to the financial output.
- **Capacity vs. Effectiveness:** Having 10,000 hours of available technical time (Capacity) is different from how well we monetize those hours (Effectiveness/Utilization).

## Who Else Is Involved
- **The Audience:** The CEO (needs to see governance/agency) and the Board (needs to see margins/scalability).    
- **The Intake Funnel:** Sales / Account Management (who drop the work on our plate).    
- **The Gatekeepers:** Solution Architects and SDMs/Project Managers (who triage, scope, and protect the engineers).    
- **The Execution Engine:** The Cloud Platform teams, L2/L3 NOC, and Infra resources listed on the Golden Sheet.
